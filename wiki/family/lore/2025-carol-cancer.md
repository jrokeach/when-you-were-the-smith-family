# Carol's Diagnosis

In March 2025, Carol (mom's mother) was diagnosed with breast cancer.

## The news

Carol called Sarah with the results. "She was so matter-of-fact about it," Sarah said. "She said 'They found something, but it's early, and we're going to fix it.' That's so Carol."

The kids learned gradually. Maya (8) was told first because she's old enough to understand "Grandma is sick but she's getting treatment."

## Treatment

Carol had surgery in April 2025, followed by chemotherapy through the summer. Michael (Uncle Mike, Carol's son) took time off to help.

The family drove to see her every few weeks during treatment. The kids made cards.

> "I drew a picture of Maggie (the dog) and wrote 'Get well soon Grandma we miss you.'" — Maya

> "I don't like when Grandma is bald," Theo (5) said. "She's still Grandma."

## The kids' understanding

- Leo (13): Quietly concerned. Did his own research. Asked thoughtful questions.
- Maya (8): Drew pictures. Asked if Grandma would die; was reassured that the doctors were optimistic.
- Theo (5): Didn't fully understand but knew "Grandma has a boo-boo in her body."
- Rosie (almost 1): Too young to understand, but noticed when video calls with Grandma showed her with a hat.

## Outcome

Carol completed treatment in October 2025. Scans showed no evidence of disease. She is in remission.

The family celebrated with a dinner at Carol's favorite restaurant.
