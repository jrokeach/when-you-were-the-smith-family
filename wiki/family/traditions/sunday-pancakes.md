# Sunday Pancakes

Every Sunday morning, whoever wakes up first makes pancakes for the family.

## Origins

Started in Austin in 2018, shortly after Leo lost his first tooth. Sarah made pancakes to celebrate, and it stuck.

"We didn't plan it as a tradition," Sarah said. "We just... kept doing it."

## The ritual

- The chef wears the "special apron" (a ridiculous one with fruits on it that Grandma Betty gave them)
- Leo usually picks the music (his taste evolved from Disney to classic rock to whatever he's into now)
- All four kids have "their" pancake shapes they request
- Rosie gets her own tiny pancake, cut into bites

## What the kids request

- Leo: "Whatever, I don't care" (but he always eats three)
- Maya: "Blueberry, but the blueberries have to be smooshed"
- Theo: "Dinosaur pancakes" (same thing every time — the dinosaur must have a long neck and tiny arms)
- Rosie: "Pancake" — she says the word now and points

## Evolution

Theo's "dinosaur pancakes" started as just circles. Then he learned what dinosaurs actually looked like, and now Sarah spends way too long on YouTube figuring out how to make a sauropod out of pancake batter.
