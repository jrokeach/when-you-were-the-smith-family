# Thunderstorms

Started around age 4 after a very loud storm overnight. Has not diminished as much as we'd expected.

## What helps

- Knowing the forecast a day in advance. The unpredictability is a big part of the fear.
- Counting seconds between lightning and thunder to estimate distance. Sam likes that this is a *math* thing, not a *feelings* thing.
- [Biscuit](../../../family/_examples/pets/biscuit.md) lying next to them.

## What doesn't

- Telling them it will pass soon.
- Reminding them they were fine last time. (They were not, in their experience, fine last time.)

## Other fears on record

- Very loud hand dryers (age 2–4, mostly outgrown).
- The dark space behind the couch (age 3, now a friend).
- As of late 2024: a new, vague fear of "things in the ocean too big to see." This one is interesting — it coexists with their [obsession with the ocean](../interests/oceans.md) rather than contradicting it.
