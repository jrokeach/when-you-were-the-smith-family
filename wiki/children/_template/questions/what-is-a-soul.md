# "What is a soul?"

Asked in the car, on the way to school, apropos of nothing. Preceded and followed by silence.

Full exchange (recorded to parent's phone right after drop-off to preserve wording):

> **Sam:** What is a soul?
> **Parent:** Where did you hear that?
> **Sam:** I just thought of it.
> **Parent:** [...tries to answer for about 90 seconds, watches Sam's face]
> **Sam:** OK.

That was the end of it. They have not brought it back up.

## Why capture this

The questions they ask unprompted are a record of what they're actually thinking about. Capture verbatim. Don't "clean up" the wording. See also [bus window quote](../quotes/2024-bus-window.md) from the same era.
