# Letter to Sam, age 6 birthday

> _This page type (`letters/`) is for letters written to the child by parents, grandparents, or other family members. Date and author each one. Read back years later, these are often the most valued pages in the KB._

---

Dear Sam,

You turned six today and it was raining and you were so happy about that. You wore the rainbow dress with your rain boots and you walked to school stepping on every puddle you could find and I watched you from behind and you did not know I was watching.

I want you to remember, because you are at an age where the world has started to feel bigger than you, that you have a way of being quiet at the right times. On the bus this summer, stuck in traffic, you said: *"everybody in those cars has their own life that's as important to them as mine is to me."* I wrote it down so I would not forget it, because I think it is the kind of thing I will want to read again.

This year: you have loved the ocean, loved Biscuit, built a submarine out of cardboard that still lives in the living room because you have banned its disposal, been scared of thunder, asked me what happens to people when they die, been the best sibling to Robin about 60% of the time, and cried twice when you had to wear jeans.

I love the person you are turning into.

— Parent
