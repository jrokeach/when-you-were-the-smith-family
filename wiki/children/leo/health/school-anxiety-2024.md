# School Anxiety, 2024

Leo started experiencing significant anxiety about school in fall 2024.

## What happened

Shortly after starting 7th grade at West Portland Middle School, Leo began saying he didn't want to go to school. At first, it seemed typical — "Monday blues." But it escalated.

> "I just feel like everyone's looking at me," he told Sarah. "Like I don't fit in."

He started having stomach aches. Missing school. Withdrawing from friends.

## What the family noticed

Sarah: "He used to be so confident. This kid who would just TALK to anyone. And then he just... stopped that. He'd come home and go straight to his room."

David: "Looking back, I think we'd been missing signs for a while. He stopped wanting to go to friend's houses. He stopped asking about video calls with people."

## Getting help

Sarah took Leo to see Dr. Patel, their pediatrician. Dr. Patel referred them to a child therapist, Jin Wei Chen, who specializes in anxiety.

Leo was resistant at first. "I didn't want to go to a 'shrink.' But she was actually cool."

## What helped

- Weekly therapy sessions
- Learning about anxiety as "what your brain does when it's trying to protect you"
- A "worry journal" where he wrote down anxious thoughts
- David taking him on more one-on-one outings
- Patience from everyone

## Current state

As of early 2026, Leo is doing better. He's formed a solid friend group. He's more confident.

> "I'm not completely better," he said. "Like, I still get nervous sometimes. But I know how to handle it now."

## Notes for the family

- Maya doesn't fully understand; they've explained that "Leo was having a hard time" without details.
- The family is now more attuned to mental health.
